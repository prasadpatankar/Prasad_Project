Policy Analysis & Research

Published Research

●    Analysis of Profits and Losses in the Indian Equity Derivatives Segment

A research study focused on the profits and losses incurred in the Indian Equity Derivatives Segment.

Period: FY22 - FY24 (Three Years)

Methodology: SEBI study analyzing P&L patterns for 1.13 crore unique individual F&O traders.

Key Findings:

  • High Loss Rate & Aggregate Loss: 93% of individual F&O traders incurred losses, with aggregate losses exceeding ₹1.8 lakh crores (USD 21 billion) over the three years.
  • Persistence: Despite consecutive years of losses, more than 75% of loss-making traders continued trading in F&O.
  • Trading Activity & Loss: Higher trading activity was associated with a higher percentage of loss-makers.
  • Options Dominance & Loss: 99.3% of traders used Options. In FY24, 91.5% of Options traders made losses.
  • Algorithmic Profits: Proprietary Traders and FPIs were net profitable, largely driven by algorithmic trading (96%-97% of their profits).
  • Younger Traders: The proportion of young traders (below 30 years) rose significantly from 31% (FY22) to 43% (FY24).
  • Small Town Participation: Individuals from ‘Small Towns’ (Beyond Top 30 cities) made up over 72% of the total F&O trader base.
  • Low-Income Growth: The proportion of “Low\_Income” traders (annual income less ₹5 lakh) increased from 71% (FY22) to 76% (FY24).
●    Investor Behaviour in Initial Public Offerings (IPOs)

Period: April 2021 - December 2023

Methodology: SEBI study analyzing 144 Main Board IPOs (92.60 lakh unique investors).

Key Findings:

  • Rapid Selling ("Flipping"): Individual investors sold 50% of shares by value within a week of listing.
  • Overall Quick Exit: 54% of all allotted shares (excluding anchor investors) were sold within a week.
  • Disposition Effect: Investors preferred selling shares that showed positive listing gains over those with losses.
  • Returns-Driven Sales: When IPO returns exceeded 20%, nearly 68% of shares were sold in a week.
  • New Investor Surge: Nearly 50% of IPO-applying Demat accounts were opened in the post-COVID period (2021-2023).
  • NII Oversubscription Halved: NII category oversubscription dropped from 38x to 17x after policy changes.
  • Big Ticket NII Drop: Large NII applications (>₹1 Cr) plummeted from ~626 to ~20 per IPO post-policy.
●    Study on extent of Hedging in the Indian EDS

A comprehensive research study focused on the transactional behavior and market structure of both institutional and retail participants in the Indian F&O segment, specifically examining hedging versus speculation activity.

Period: April 1, 2024 – March 1, 2025

Methodology: Analysis of transaction and holding level data for over 10 million unique F&O traders to identify structural patterns and trends in positioning.

Key Findings:

  • Structural Divergence: Mutual Funds (MFs) consistently maintained a net short position (for hedging/arbitrage) using ~2.5% of their equity portfolio, while individual traders were consistently net long (utilizing 15-20% of their overall equity portfolio).
  • Minimal Hedging by Individuals: Only approx. 2-3% of individuals attempted any form of hedging, with direct, contra-position hedging accounting for a negligible 0.02% of their total F&O trading activity.
  • Dominance of Speculation: The vast majority of retail activity was short-term: 68-73% focused on intraday trades, and 25-30% took explicit speculative positions.
  • Equity Disconnect: A significant portion of F&O traders lacked substantial equity holdings: 33% had zero underlying equity position, and over 90% had total securities account balances below INR 1,000,000.

  • --> Contributed to the implementation of SEBI’s Unified Distilled File Formats (UDiFF) initiative, successfully transitioning market data reporting to a harmonized, standardized system compliant with international ISO standards.
